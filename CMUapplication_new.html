Interface Overflow, 2025, Variable, Video Game made by Unreal Engine 5

Interface Overflow is a work-in-progress game that turns UI, an element often treated as invisible background support, into the main protagonist. Instead of using UI simply to explain a world, the world itself is built from interfaces. The screen becomes the space you navigate, and the interface actively shapes what you notice, believe, and do.

So far, the project includes only the first level, designed as a starting point for a larger series. It introduces the core idea by pushing the player into a space of saturation and interference, where navigation and decision-making are constantly redirected by the interface. Upcoming levels will extend these experiments, such as printing the player's keyboard inputs directly onto the screen.

Camera Annihilation, 2025, Variable, Video Essay

Camera Annihilation is a video essay that interrogates the symbolic and political functions of the camera within contemporary surveillance structures. By dissecting the act of image-making itself, the work proposes a provocative thesis: anything can become a camera under systems of power and observation. Through performative gestures and critical montage, it reflects on how cameras do not merely record reality—they shape it, discipline it, and, ultimately, weaponize it.

The piece critiques the entangled relationship between visibility, control, and technology in the age of ubiquitous surveillance. It employs game sequences and glitch aesthetics to procedurally "destroy" surveillance cameras, transforming viewers from passive observers into active agents of resistance, further complicating the power dynamics between viewer, subject, and machine.

Presented at the SAIC Spring 2025 Undergraduate Exhibition.

Su Casa, 2024, Variable, Video Game made by Unity

Su Casa is a cozy decorating game made in Unity that turns domestic comfort into an absurd spectacle of excess. Pressing the space bar causes random furniture to fall continuously from the sky, and the player can use the mouse to drag every object anywhere in the room. What begins as playful home styling quickly becomes a chaotic pileup, as the space is buried under an unstoppable stream of commodities and the floor transforms into something closer to a ruin. By removing limits from the game's internal economy, Su Casa makes greed visible and physical, showing how unchecked accumulation overwhelms care, order, and the idea of "home."

Your Oyster, 2024, Variable, Video Game made by Unity

Your Oyster is a Unity-made game that invites the player to witness a miniature planet across vast stretches of time. Pressing Q and E shifts the terrain and lighting, transforming the environment in sweeping cycles that feel like centuries passing in seconds. Pressing G switches into a god's-eye view, widening the perspective and emphasizing the distance between the observer and the world below. Rather than controlling a character, the player manipulates conditions and watches landscapes rise and erode, shadows stretch and collapse, and the planet reorganize itself through time. Your Oyster frames worldbuilding as observation instead of conquest, turning geological change into something tangible, playable, and quietly uncanny.

Watching 3D Movie Without Glasses, 2023

Watching 3D Movie Without Glasses is my first piece made in Unreal Engine. It started from a simple thought: in reality we see the world with two eyes, but in most games everything is rendered through a single camera. In Unreal Engine, I placed two cameras offset along the y-axis, captured both views, and composited them into one image, creating a misaligned, faux-3D, glitch-like effect that feels like watching a 3D movie without wearing the glasses.

The landfill, 2023. Digital, variable

Landfill is a real-time Unreal Engine project inspired by a 2010s-era game asset that built convincing foliage from two intersecting planes with 2D textures, producing a glitchy yet strangely complete form. To extend that surreal language, I generated foliage textures with DALL·E 2 and introduced oversized fruit as unexpected digital monuments, using a list of fruits sourced from ChatGPT as the basis for the models. The AI images were brought into the pipeline through Blender and assembled in Unreal, where Blueprints automate key steps such as cutting out backgrounds, shaping the planes, and creating translucency.

Orange Chicken, 2022, Variable, AI generated image

Orange Chicken is an image generated with the DALL·E 2 model from the simple prompt "orange chicken." Instead of producing a straightforward food photograph, the model returned an absurd hybrid that resembles Americanized Chinese cuisine, a chicken's head fused onto an orange. The image becomes a visual metaphor for a dish that is culturally unstable. Orange chicken is not a common food in China, yet in the United States it is marketed as a signature "Chinese" favorite. In this work, the AI's literal misunderstanding mirrors a broader process of translation and invention, where a name hardens into an identity and a commercial fantasy takes on the appearance of authenticity. The resulting creature points to a culture that is manufactured rather than inherited, and to an object that feels real precisely because it is repeatedly generated, circulated, and consumed.
